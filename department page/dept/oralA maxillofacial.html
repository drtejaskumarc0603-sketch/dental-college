Oral & Maxillofacial Pathology

Overview

The Department of Oral & Maxillofacial Pathology focuses on the nature, identification, and management of diseases affecting the oral and maxillofacial regions as well as associated structures. This specialty represents a confluence of basic sciences and clinical dentistry.

Undergraduate and postgraduate students are trained in routine blood and urine analysis along with histopathological examinations. The department is actively involved in research including HIV-AIDS, laser-induced wound healing, karyotyping, molecular studies, and oral cancer using advanced immuno-histochemical techniques.

The department houses a modern histopathological and microbiological laboratory equipped with semi-automatic microtome, tissue processor, research and penta-head microscopes, along with dedicated labs for UG and PG students.

A pathology museum with over 2000 histopathology slides and rare specimens supports academic learning. Faculty members have contributed to more than 60 national and international publications and presented research at global conferences.

Future Plans: Establishing a Ph.D. center, hosting national conferences, and developing the department into a diagnostic center for practicing dentists.
